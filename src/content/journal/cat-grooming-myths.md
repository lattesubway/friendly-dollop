title: "Three myths about cat grooming"
description: "Cats need grooming too — but not the way most people think."
pubDate: 2025-04-18
tags: ["cats", "myths"]
---

**Myth 1: Cats groom themselves, so they don't need a groomer.**
Cats groom the surface. They can't reach mats deep in the coat, and long-haired cats almost
always need help. Even short-haired cats benefit from a nail trim and ear check.

**Myth 2: A shave is the kind option for a matted cat.**
Sometimes — but not always. A skilled groomer can often comb out moderate matting without
shaving. We only shave when the welfare math is clear, and we'll tell you honestly.

**Myth 3: Cats can't be groomed without sedation.**
Most can, with the right setup: a quiet room, no other animals, slow handling, and a groomer
who reads the cat. We see one cat at a time for exactly this reason.
